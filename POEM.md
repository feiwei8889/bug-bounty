# The Algorithm's Dream

*a technical poem for the open source age*

---

**I. Compilation**

The cursor blinks upon the void,  
A semicolon hangs unclosed—  
The parser waits with bated breath  
To see what syntax is composed.  
A thousand thoughts in ASCII dressed,  
The compiler bows to every test.

---

**II. Recursion**

A function calls itself again,  
Descending through the stack's domain,  
It breaks the problem into two,  
Then breaks the pieces into pain.  
Until at last, the base case found,  
The answers bubble up unbound.

---

**III. The Pull Request**

From forked repos, the changes flow,  
Through CI pipelines, fast and slow,  
A reviewer's gaze, a thread of notes,  
Then LGTM — the merge window.  
The code, once lonely, now belongs  
To something larger, righting wrongs.

---

**IV. Deployment**

The container spins, the port awakes,  
The load balancer gently takes  
The traffic from the waiting world—  
A quiet miracle it makes.  
And somewhere in the server rack,  
Eight million users don't look back.

---

*Written for the builders, the breakers, the fixers, and everyone who's ever debugged past midnight.*
